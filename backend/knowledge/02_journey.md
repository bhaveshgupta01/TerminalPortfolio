# My Journey

## From Delhi to New York: Following the Hub Effect
I was drawn to Delhi first — it's a hub for India, a center of energy, opportunity, and learning. New York applies the same lens on a global scale: it's the financial and industrial capital of the world. NYU Courant isn't a random choice for me — it's the institution that influences major activities across NYC, which made it the natural gravitational center to build scalable, impactful technology.

## Technical Evolution: Android → ML → Domain-Specific AI

**1. Android Era.** I started building Android apps. While shipping them, I hit a limitation — I needed *intelligent search*. That gap pushed me toward ML.

**2. ML Era.** Once I learned ML, a new question opened up: which *domains* benefit most from machine learning? Healthcare showed me the opportunity first (I published a deep transfer learning paper on mammography classification and built a drug-target prediction model at Synopsys), but the deeper insight was universal — *any* domain benefits from ML when applied thoughtfully.

**3. Current Era.** Financial markets, healthcare, enterprise systems, autonomous trading — I'm exploring where AI can solve real, tangible problems. Not generic AI tools. Specific solutions to specific problems across industries.

## What Excites Me Professionally
- AI/ML with depth, not surface-level applications
- Model finetuning and learning how to build custom AI
- AI-assisted development — leveraging GenAI to turn ideas into working projects
- Projects with visible impact where my contribution is highlighted
- Cross-domain exploration: healthcare, finance, enterprise

## 5-Year Vision
Building AI systems that solve real problems across industries, where the impact is measurable and my contribution is visible. The direction is clear even if the exact path isn't mapped.
